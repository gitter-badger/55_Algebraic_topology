 \begin{definition}\textbf{The Fourier-Stieltjes algebra of a groupoid, $G_l$}.
In ref. \cite{PALT2k3}), A.L.T. Paterson defined the \emph{Fourier-Stieltjes algebra of a 
groupoid}, $G_l$, as the space of coefficients $\phi = (\xi,\eta)$, where $\xi,\eta$ are \\
$L^{\infty}$-sections for some measurable $G_l$ -Hilbert bundle $(\mu,\Re,L)$. Thus, for $x \in G_l$,
\begin{equation}
\phi(x) = L(x) \xi (s(x),\eta (r(x))).
\end{equation}
Therefore, $\phi$ belongs to $L^\infty{G_l} = L^\infty({G_l},\nu)$.
\end{definition}
