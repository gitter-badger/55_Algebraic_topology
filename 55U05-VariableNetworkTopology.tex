\begin{definition}  

 \emph{A variable network topology} is defined by an indexed family
or class of networks of one-dimensional $CW$-complexes $[CW_i]$ with $i \in I$ in the category \textbf{Top} of topological spaces with additional homology (or cohomology) axioms, rules, or properties of the underlying $CW$-complexes, that specify the transformation maps as a sequence of homotopoy maps.
\end{definition}
